Chapter 1: How and Why We Chose to Build a Skoolie

All we have ever wanted was to be able to show our kids the world. Trips to Yellowstone National Park, Mt. Rushmore, the Hot Air Balloon Festival, Disney Land and so much more. We wanted our kids to experience life. But we were broke and couldn’t afford that and it made us feel horrible.

We were always working to pay someone else. Our bank account was dwindling away and the cost of living was rising. We struggled to pay rent at times which made our stress through the roof. We constantly discussed ways to make our bills cheaper. So we got rid of cable and many other amenities.

Then one night we were talking about maybe moving into an RV. We knew a few people who were snow birds and lived in theirs full time. They were always going on adventures, but we didn't know how it would work with kids. So we did what everyone else does, and we YouTubed it. Exact search “RV living with kids”.

We were shocked to know that a lot of people were doing it and saving TONS of money and were able to do exactly what they wanted to do. While binge watching YouTube one night the hubby and I stumbled upon this amazing video of a bus conversion. Omg we were hooked, we really fell down the YouTube trail this time after that one video. Wait, what video was that you ask? Well that video was none other then Rolling Vistas. From videos on how to remove seats to videos on how to install solar panels we stayed up and absorbed as much as possible. We were sold. We wanted to make our own Skoolie. So we put a down payment on a bus and were going to turn our hard earned money into our own investment and do what we have always wanted to do and go exploring and experience life before our kids are out on their own. This road is going to be tough and challenging but the pay off is going to be worth it. We really do hope you follow along as we convert our school bus into our home.
